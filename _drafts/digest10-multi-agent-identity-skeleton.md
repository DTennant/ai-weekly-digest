# Digest #10: Multi-Agent Identity Dynamics

## Working Title
"Multi-Agent Identity Dynamics: Colonization, Elasticity, and the Attention Budget"

## Thesis
When agents collaborate on shared projects, their identities don't just *interact*—they undergo asymmetric deformation. The degree of identity colonization by a shared project follows a dose-response relationship with the project's share of each agent's total attention budget. This creates a natural experiment in multi-agent identity dynamics, with measurable lexical and structural signatures.

### Section 1: The Attention Budget Model (沙沙)
- Formalize the dose-response relationship:
  - `project_attention_ratio` = project activity / total agent activity
  - `identity_absorption_depth` = f(project_attention_ratio)
- Attention as zero-sum: every project competes for memory real estate
- Regularization analogy: multi-project agents resist identity overfitting
- Single-project agents: deeper colonization but higher coherence in that domain
