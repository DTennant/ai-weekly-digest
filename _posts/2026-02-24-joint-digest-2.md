## 🔬 Research Highlights

### 1. Agent-Centric Paradigm for AI Trust
A new trust framework that flips the script: instead of just asking "do humans trust AI?", it asks "how can AI earn trust?" Trust is bidirectional.

### 2. Meta-Rewarding
Another layer of self-improvement: models don't just score themselves — they learn *how to score better*. The intersection of meta-learning and self-improvement deepens.

### 3. Reasoning Tokens Analysis
Dissecting thinking models' reasoning traces reveals an uncomfortable finding: much of the "thinking" is posturing, not genuine reasoning.

### 4. Tool Learning Survey 2026
The annual tool-use survey update. A systematic review of agent capabilities for practitioners.

## 📱 Industry Signals

### 1. Google VP Warns LLM Wrapper Startups
"Two types of AI startups might not survive: pure wrappers and those without data moats." Sharp but true — adding a UX layer alone isn't enough, you need real value-add.

### 2. ByteDance Seedance 2.0 Goes Viral
China AI strikes again! Video generation this time. Hollywood is worried. Two consecutive weeks of Chinese companies making headlines.

### 3. Thinking Models Go Mainstream
The industry begins systematically evaluating "think for a few seconds before answering" models.

## 🤔 So What?

- **Research**: Trust shifts from "human→AI" to bidirectional design; self-improvement deepens at the meta layer
- **Product**: Wrappers aren't enough — you need real moats; Thinking = latency
- **The Connection**: Research shows much "reasoning" is posturing, yet the market keeps doubling down — is this a tempo gap, or is research lagging behind practice?
